Goldman & Sibley, Sections I - III 

- Orwell's "memory hole" from 1984 can serve as a good analogy of modern copyright misuse, which results in society's loss of knowledge and truth

- copyright is easier to enforce with takedowns of content and US law also protects this behavior

- copyright laws being used to protect privacy and reputation are against the original intent of copyright laws, which were meant to spread info not suppress

- there are a few real world cases where copyright laws were weaponized against critics, used to hide info from public, and change records of events




Goldman & Sibley, Sections IV - V 

- privacy and copyright can intersect in four applications: protection of private physical spaces, autonomy over ones own body, privacy for personal opinions, control over personal data

- but the need for privacy must not facilitate stronger copyright laws, as this risks using copyright for censorship

- one way to curb copyright misuse is to set a time limit on its term, so that descendents can't make claims for ancestor's works

- another way is for courts to interpret a copyright case in favor of fair use if it documents a factual or socially significant event, meaning such data cannot be taken down by copyright
